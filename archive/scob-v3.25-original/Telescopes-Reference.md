# Science Centre Observatory (SCOB) — Telescope Reference

Singapore Science Centre Observatory, Jurong (1.3342°N, 103.7357°E, 15 m a.m.s.l.).
This is the standing reference for instruments available at the Friday public stargazing sessions.

## Fixed (dome) telescopes

| Instrument | Aperture | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main telescope | 40 cm (16") | Cassegrain reflector, focal length 520 cm | In the 5.5 m stainless-steel dome on an equatorial mount (English yoke). Equipped with a digital camera for astrophotography. The showpiece instrument. |
| Sub-telescope (on main) | 15 cm (6") | Apochromatic Kepler refractor, focal length 180 cm | Piggybacked on the 40 cm; wide-field, high-contrast views. |
| Second dome (primary) | 11" (28 cm) | Catadioptric | Houses the observatory's second-largest scope. |
| Second dome (secondary) | 7" (18 cm) | Coupled to the 11" | Paired for a second viewing station. |

## Portable telescopes (set up on the viewing deck)

Brought out for public sessions to spread crowds across multiple instruments. Typical units:

| Instrument | Aperture | Type | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Celestron CPC series (e.g. CPC 800 / 925 / 1100) | 8"–11" (20–28 cm) | Schmidt-Cassegrain (SCT), computerised alt-az fork GoTo (no GPS module — date/time/location keyed in manually) | Moon, planets, bright clusters; fast GoTo for queues |
| Celestron C6 | 6" (15 cm) | Schmidt-Cassegrain (SCT) | Moon and bright planets; light, quick to aim |
| Other portable refractors / Dobsonians | varies | refractor / Newtonian | Wide-field views, overflow stations |

## Matching targets to instruments

- **Moon & bright planets:** any instrument; the portable CPC/C6 SCTs are ideal for high-throughput public queues.
- **Lunar terminator detail, planetary surface features:** the 40 cm (16") main scope or the 11" for maximum resolution.
- **Globular cluster cores (e.g. Omega Centauri), double stars:** 40 cm main scope resolves them best; the 6" apo refractor gives crisp, high-contrast star fields.
- **Faint deep-sky (nebulae, galaxies):** the 40 cm — but only on dark, moonless, low-haze nights.

*Sources: Science Centre Singapore (Stargazing / Observatory pages); Achievers Dream observatory profile; Celestron CPC & C6 specifications.*
