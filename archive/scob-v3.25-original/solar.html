Safe-viewing checklist

  • Full-aperture solar filter (white-light or H-alpha) fitted & secured on the front of the tube.
  • Finder scope capped or removed.
  • Inspect the filter for pinholes or scratches before every use — discard if damaged.
  • Never leave the scope unattended while pointed near the Sun.
  • For projection instead of filters: only with a suitable scope, and never let anyone look through the eyepiece.

What to look for

  • White-light (filtered): dark sunspots and spot groups drifting across the disc over days; bright faculae near the edge; the mottled granulation (the Sun's boiling surface) in steady seeing.
  • Hydrogen-alpha (H-α scope): flame-like prominences arcing off the edge, dark thread-like filaments, bright plages and the textured chromosphere.
  • Sunspot numbers rise and fall over an ~11-year cycle — some days the disc is busy, others blank. Check spaceweather.com for today's activity.

SCOB solar setup

Use a white-light filter on the deck CPC / C6 for sunspots, or a dedicated H-α scope for prominences. Track with the mount's Solar rate (Menu → Tracking → Rate → Solar). Keep the queue moving — the view drifts, so re-centre often.
