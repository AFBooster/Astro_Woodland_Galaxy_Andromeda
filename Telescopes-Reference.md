# Andromeda Observatory (Woodlands Galaxy CC) — Telescope Reference

Andromeda Observatory, Woodlands Galaxy Community Club, 31 Woodlands Avenue 6, Singapore 738991 (1.43901°N, 103.80266°E, level 6). This is the standing reference for the instruments used at the public stargazing sessions.

## Confirmed instruments

| Instrument | Aperture | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Andromeda dome telescope | 30 cm (12") | Reflector | The observatory's main telescope, under the level-6 dome — the showpiece for the Moon, planets and brighter deep-sky objects. Its exact optical sub-design (Newtonian vs Cassegrain) and mount type are not publicly documented; **confirm with the observatory**. |
| Takahashi Sky 90 (rooftop) | 90 mm (3.5") | Fluorite apochromatic refractor, f/5.6 (≈500 mm focal length) | Set up on the rooftop for the public. Wide-field, high-contrast, beginner-friendly and astrophotography-capable. Typically more than one unit. |

## Matching targets to instruments

- **Moon & bright planets (public queue):** the rooftop Takahashi Sky 90 — fast to aim, wide, forgiving, ideal for a moving line.
- **Planetary detail, lunar terminator, globular-cluster cores, double stars:** the 30 cm dome reflector, at higher power, for maximum resolution.
- **Wide-field targets (large open clusters, bright nebulae, the Milky Way):** the Sky 90's wide true field frames them beautifully.
- **Faint deep-sky (galaxies, faint nebulae):** the 30 cm dome — but only on dark, moonless, low-haze nights.
